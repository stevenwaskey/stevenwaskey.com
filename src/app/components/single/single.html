Hi.
I'm Steven Waskey.

  I'm a lucky guy married to an amazing woman. I'm 35 years old, but I prefer to only act my age when I really have to. I'm motivated by people. I love good energy & I love growth. I'm fueled by the excitement of challenge. I'm intrigued by the thought behind design; whether it's the designer, or the designer's audience I'm into at the moment. I love process refinement & implementation. Maybe that's why I got into software engineering. My current personal goal to obtain my private pilot license. I've completed ground school, but still need to tackle flight hours. (IFR, I'm coming for you.) I enjoy trying to make web-apps more performant. Why Hello, performance analytics. Where have you been my whole life? I have two dogs, Cooper & Bentley. Cooper's rambunctious, always. Bentley is probably sleeping on one of my pillows at this very moment. I always choose to go the extra mile. Details matter. I enjoy competition. If you're open to learn from every loss, then in the end, you'll always win. I shoot archery, but don't get to near as much these days as I used to. (That'll change again someday.) Nothing can replace being outside in Florida on a sunny day.
  My first web project was in 1996. My father owned an internet company (called Sunny, FL Internet) when I was younger. I thought I was hot-shit rolling up to a T1 at work. I spent a few years doing graphics, enjoyed it, but ultimately didn't feel fulfilled from it. I needed to keep on the journey. I recognized that to increase my value as a web guy, I needed to be better at the dev side. So I started programming a little. I loved it. It was very black & white, true or false, on or off ... There were no blurred lines. You either hit the mark, or you didn't. After a 5 or 6 years of bustin' my ass, I got burnt out. I spent a few years working in a power-sports shop. What a blast for a kid who loved riding dirt. This was where I started to learn people, and effective communications, really working as a team member - and I got to ride a lot of cool bikes.

  After about 5 years in power-sports world, I made my way back to tech. Immediately feeling more empowered. I jumped head first back into dev & engineering. I was very fortunate to meet some sharp people & got to work on a few really fun projects. Then, in 09, I started with with Mid Florida Community Services, Inc. MFCS's mission was to help poverty-stricken families reach self-sufficiency. Over the next 6 years, I developed all of our internal CRM applications. It started as a very basic intake system to archive data & spit it back out on PDF. It grew to house millions & millions of clients records over it's lifespan. It helped keep electricity on for over 100,000 people in poverty. It was something I was very passionate about - I love the idea of building technology that helps families find their footing for a better life. It was an incredible experience designing apps for people (mostly, not all) that have no tech savvy. I was terrible at UI/UX when I started, but it was profound what the challenges taught me. There's a bunch more I could tell you, and I love talking about it .... so if you're bored, I can take care of that.

  I left MFCS in 2015 to start Atlas Results. I simply wanted to write bad-ass web-apps to streamline business processes, and do it with elegant interfaces. The challenge of trying to perfectly identify how to visually communicate your message or process is a journey without a destination. There is no end. You can only get better.
